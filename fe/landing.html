The Current Crisis

Why Traditional Healthcare IT Fails

Centralized databases create single points of failure. The consequences are real and dangerous.

Life-Threatening Delays

Emergency transfers can take over 174 minutes for stroke patients because hospitals cannot share data instantly.

lock_open

Massive Data Breaches

Centralized servers are hacker targets. In 2023 alone, 133 million records were stolen in cyberattacks[cite: 182].

edit_off

Silent Data Tampering

Standard databases allow records to be edited or deleted silently. There is no guarantee that your medical history hasn't been altered[cite: 186].

The Solution

MedChain: Trustless Integrity.

We solve these problems by moving away from centralized servers. MedChain uses a Private Blockchain where data is permanent, and access is controlled by code, not people.

policy

Smart Access Control (Chaincode)

Doctors can only view records if the patient has granted permission via a smart contract. No permission = No access[cite: 270].

cloud_done

Secure Off-Chain Storage (IPFS)

Large files (MRI, X-Rays) are encrypted and stored on IPFS. Only the unique "fingerprint" (Hash) is stored on the blockchain[cite: 274].

history_edu

Immutable Audit Trail

Every time a record is viewed or uploaded, it is permanently logged. This history cannot be deleted or changed[cite: 278].
